Suzuki Lessons

For children younger than 8 years old wanting to learn violin, I recommend that they start with the Suzuki method. One of the tenants of the Suzuki philosophy is parent involvement in the child's practice. From a developmental standpoint, the vast majority of children below the age of 8 need the critical thinking skills of their practice partner in order to be successful at learning the violin. I reccommend that every parent interested in beginning Suzuki violin lessons read "Nurtured by Love" by Shinichi Suzuki and research the Suzuki method before enrolling their child. The Suzuki Method requires a strong commitment on the part of the parent and the child; while sometimes challenging, those that rise to meet the challenge are greatly rewarded.

Suzuki Talent Education Association of Australia (Victoria) Inc has more in-depth information. You can also contact me with further questions.

Studio Policy

Private Lessons are given weekly in 15, 30, 45 and 60 minute lengths. Private lessons focus primarily on technique and repertoire as well as musicianship and creative problem-solving/practice skills. Dr. Suzuki is often quoted as saying that lessons help to build a child's overall character as well.

Practice

Regular home practice is essential for student success. Ideally, students should aim for at least 5 days of practice each week for the same length of time as your lesson.

Short, daily practice sessions are more effective than one or two long sessions per week. The most important time for students to practice is right after their weekly lesson, to solidify what they have just learned. Research has shown that retention of new knowledge drops to 60% after just 24 hours.

Parental involvement in home practice is crucial for student success. Please help your student establish and stick to a practice routine, and see that practice time is free of distractions.

It is imperative that the parent available during the lesson is also available during practice time to make sure that the student is practicing correctly!

Attendance

Students are expected to attend each scheduled week of the year. When you register for lessons, your timeslot is reserved for you for the teaching year. Please keep in mind that my studio operates at full capacity and make-ups for missed lessons are not guaranteed.

No refunds will be given for missed lessons.In the event that your student needs to miss a lesson because of a conflict or illness, please contact me with as much advance notice as possible (at least 24 hours for non-emergency/illness situations). Each family is responsible for contacting me to reserve an available make-up time.

After three (3) unexcused absences, I reserve the right to remove the student(s) from my studio. An unexcused absence is defined as not providing 24 hours' notice before an absence. In the case of illness or family emergency, the 24 hours' notice policy is waived.

Any lessons missed by the instructor due to illness, emergency, or performance will be rescheduled as soon as possible.

Please be prompt - lesson times cannot be extended for students who arrive late.

Summer lessons are scheduled on a by-the-lesson basis. Students should expect to continue lessons on at least a part-time schedule through the summer to maintain the skills they develop through the school year. Students who do not enroll for summer lessons are not guaranteed a spot in the studio for the next year.

Equipment

All students must have their own instrument for home practice. The quality of your student's home instrument plays a significant role in their potential for success.

Students are required to purchase their own materials, which includes a notebook and any necessary music/equipment. Students must bring their instrument, music, and notebook to each lesson.

Tuition

Tuition is based on a 9 month (September through June) school year, which has been calculated to include two weeks of winter break and one week of Thanksgiving break (tuition rates won't change those months). Students are expected to sign up for 6 lessons throughout the summer (June-August) in order to retain their spot in the studio. Scheduling and payment for the summer will be decided before the end of May.

Students should plan to commit to lessons for the full school year. If unforeseen circumstances require a student to terminate lessons mid-year, one month's paid, written notice is required to end lessons.
